📘 How the Analyzer Works

This tool evaluates your LinkedIn draft using four complementary models. Here's exactly what each score measures and how it's calculated.

🎯 AIDA Score (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

What it measures: How well your post grabs attention, maintains interest, builds desire, and drives action.

How we calculate: Each component is scored 0–100 based on:

  • Attention: Presence of a question, numbers, strong opening.
  • Interest: Personal stories, data, paragraph structure.
  • Desire: Storytelling, policy relevance, evidence.
  • Action: Call-to-action phrases, discussion triggers.

Final AIDA Score = average of the four components.

💡 Thought Leadership Score

What it measures: Originality, credibility, and conversation-starting potential.

Dimensions (0–25 each):

  • Original Insight
  • Personal Experience
  • Evidence / Data
  • Discussion Trigger

Total (0–100): 0-40 Generic · 41-70 Informative · 71-85 Thought Leadership · 86-100 Signature Insight.

🏛 Policy Communication Score

What it measures: How effectively your post communicates policy issues.

Five components (0–20 each): Problem · Evidence · Analysis · Policy Implication · Solution.

Total (0–100): Based on keyword detection and structural analysis.

📈 Predictive Engagement Score

What it measures: Estimated post performance based on a regression model.

Formula: y = β₀ + β₁(post length) + β₂(paragraphs) + β₃(hashtags) + β₄(sentiment) + β₅(personal story) + β₆(data presence) + β₇(question) + β₈(CTA).

Coefficients are pre-set based on typical engagement patterns. The result is scaled to a 0–100% engagement score.

💡 Pro tip: Use the toggles under the input box to manually adjust AIDA detections and see how it affects your scores.
